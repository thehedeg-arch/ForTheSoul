## Sites

- **[Early Works](https://www.early.works/?ref=siteinspire)** — Minimalist yet elegant typography; layered background typography and sketches on scroll that add a handwritten, personal, and "caring" touch.
- **[Signal Studio](https://signal-a.studio/)** — Cohesive and elegant mix of typographic styles (combining bold, italicized, and regular inline text seamlessly).
- **[Jason Bergh](https://www.jasonbergh.com/)** — Engaging loading sequence leading into an immersive background video in the hero section.
- **[Valiente Brands](https://valientebrands.com/)** — Artistic text layering that partially overlaps images and spans across the page. *Idea to carry forward*: Layering muted, large background text (e.g., "truth and grace") in a distinct font style, kept separate from the main readable text.

## Patterns to carry forward

- **Large muted background typography** — a single word ("TRUTH", "GRACE") at 3–5% opacity in Gloock, used as textural wallpaper behind foreground content (Valiente Brands + Early Works)
- **Typographic mixing inline** — combining Gloock italic + Jost bold + regular weight in a single composed headline; the arrangement is part of the design (Signal Studio)
- **Cinematic full-screen hero** — background video or simulated atmospheric gradient; the site earns attention before demanding it (Jason Bergh)
- **Text overlapping imagery** — headlines that bleed across or behind image/video panels at editorial scale
- **Scroll-triggered reveals** — content appears as the user descends; CSS + IntersectionObserver only
- **Personal, unhurried pace** — whitespace and restraint over density; the feeling of a book, not a feed

## Patterns to avoid

- Heavy animation libraries or JS-dependent motion
- Generic church aesthetic (stock photography, cross imagery, bright blue backgrounds)
- Cluttered heroes — one dominant idea per section
- Dark backgrounds with bright white text at full opacity — use seashell (warm off-white) at controlled opacity instead
